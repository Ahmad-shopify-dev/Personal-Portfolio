Building Accessible Shopify Themes: Best Practices for Inclusive Design

As a Shopify developer, creating themes that are visually appealing and functional is only part of the job. Ensuring those themes are accessible to all users, including those with disabilities, is a critical responsibility. In 2025, accessibility is not just a legal requirement but also a way to reach a wider audience and boost conversions. In this blog, I’ll share my best practices for building accessible Shopify themes, making your store inclusive and user-friendly for everyone.

Why Accessibility Matters in Shopify Themes

Accessibility ensures that your store can be used by people with visual, auditory, motor, or cognitive impairments. By following standards like the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG), you create a better experience for all users while improving SEO and complying with regulations like the ADA. As a developer, I prioritize accessibility to help merchants build trust, reduce bounce rates, and make their stores welcoming to the 1 in 4 adults with disabilities.

5. Make Forms Accessible

Forms, like checkout or newsletter sign-ups, need clear labels and error handling. I ensure every input has a corresponding label and use ARIA for errors. Example:



Please enter a valid email.

I also validate inputs with JavaScript and display errors in a way screen readers can announce.

6. Test with Accessibility Tools

I test themes using tools like:

  • Wave: To check for missing alt text or ARIA issues.
  • NVDA: To simulate screen reader navigation.
  • Browser DevTools: To inspect focus order and contrast.

I also test on real devices and consult with users who rely on assistive technologies to catch real-world issues.

7. Document for Merchants

To maintain accessibility, I provide merchants with guidelines on adding content, like writing descriptive alt text or choosing high-contrast colors in the Theme Editor. This ensures the theme stays accessible over time.

Tips for Accessible Shopify Themes

  1. Plan Accessibility Early: Build it into the design phase to avoid costly retrofits.
  2. Keep It Simple: Avoid overcomplicating layouts, which can confuse assistive tools.
  3. Stay WCAG-Compliant: Aim for Level AA compliance as a minimum standard.
  4. Educate Clients: Teach merchants how to maintain accessibility when updating content.
  5. Stay Updated: Follow Shopify’s accessibility updates and new WCAG guidelines.
